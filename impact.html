Impact & Solutions

Evaluating the societal fallout of digital manipulation and forging a path forward.

"The widespread use of AI-generated misinformation and disinformation, and the tools to disseminate it, is the greatest short-term threat to the global economy and democratic stability."
— World Economic Forum, Global Risks Report 2024

Societal Effects: The Cost of Inaction

AI-driven disinformation is no longer just a digital nuisance; it is a profound societal threat that degrades institutions, financial markets, and public safety.

Erosion of Democratic Trust

When voters can no longer distinguish between genuine candidate statements and synthetic deepfakes, overall participation and trust in the electoral process sharply decline. 1

Financial Sabotage

AI audio cloning has successfully impersonated corporate executives, authorizing millions of dollars in fraudulent wire transfers and briefly crashing specific stock values. 2

Policy Recommendations

Addressing a threat of this magnitude requires a coordinated, multi-stakeholder approach. Based on our empirical findings, we propose the following three-pillar framework:

01
Tech Industry Legislative

Mandatory Cryptographic Watermarking

Legislatures must require all commercial Generative AI platforms to embed invisible, unalterable cryptographic watermarks into their outputs. This allows browsers to automatically label synthetic media.

02
Legislative

Algorithmic Liability Laws

Amend current internet safe-harbor laws so that social media platforms hold liability if their specific recommendation algorithms actively accelerate the spread of known, damaging deepfakes.

03
Civil Society Government Grants

"Pre-bunking" Public Education

Shift funding from traditional "fact-checking" to "pre-bunking" campaigns. Educate the public on exactly *how* deepfakes are made, inoculating them against future psychological manipulation.
