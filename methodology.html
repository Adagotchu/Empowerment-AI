Empowering users with the tools to identify artificial content and secure their digital presence.

How to Spot Fake Content

As AI models become more sophisticated, the "Uncanny Valley"—that feeling that something is almost human but not quite—is shrinking. We must look for specific technical artifacts.

To identify AI-generated media, use the **S.I.F.T.** method: Stop, Investigate the source, Find better coverage, and Trace claims back to the original context. Beyond that, look for these physical "glitches" in AI generation:

Visual Inconsistencies

Look at the edges: AI often struggles with hair against backgrounds, earring symmetry, and the fine details of human hands (often generating 6 fingers or warped jewelry).

Unnatural Speech Patterns

In AI audio, listen for a lack of breathing sounds or "micro-hesitations" (like 'um' or 'ah') that humans naturally use. AI speech often feels perfectly paced but emotionally flat.

Protecting Yourself Online

Detection is only half the battle. You must also proactively shield your digital footprint to prevent being targeted by automated disinformation campaigns.

1

Verify Before Sharing

Disinformation relies on emotional triggers. If a post makes you feel instant rage or panic, it is likely designed to do so. Wait 60 seconds and check a secondary reputable source.

2

Audit Your Social Privacy

Bot networks scrape "Public" profiles to find targets for personalized disinformation. Lock your social media accounts to "Friends Only" to prevent being added to mass-tagging bot lists.

3

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Protect your identity from being stolen for "Persona Generation." Using MFA ensures that even if a bot scrapes your credentials, they cannot hijack your account to spread lies in your name.
